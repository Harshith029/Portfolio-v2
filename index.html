01 / About

2026AI / Fine-tuning

Nemotron Reasoning LoRA

LoRA adapter development and evaluation for NVIDIA's Nemotron reasoning model — with synthetic data generators and eval harnesses for bit manipulation, substitution ciphers, and algebraic puzzles.

Applied ML insight Naïve LoRA fine-tuning actually hurt baseline performance. The real leverage was in data distribution analysis, not more training.
LoRAPyTorchHuggingFaceSynthetic Data
The lesson: in applied ML, fine-tuning is the easy part. Understanding why the model is failing — and fixing the data, not the recipe — is where engineers earn their keep.

AITO — AI Traffic Optimizer

An AI-assisted routing system for city-scale traffic. Distributes vehicles across viable routes using road capacity, school-timing patterns, and vehicle-type priorities to prevent bottlenecks before they form.

Real-world target Built for Hyderabad's traffic patterns. Usable by commuters, delivery platforms, and urban planners.

FAULTLINE

A pre-incident cascade-failure detector for microservice architectures. Combines deterministic drift signals with Bedrock-powered root-cause hypotheses to flag problems before they propagate.

Engineering constraint Designed under a ~$120 cloud budget cap. Expensive model calls only fire on real anomalies; cheap math handles the baseline.
LambdaDynamoDBBedrockAPI Gateway

AI Agents With Real Guardrails

Most agent demos skip rollback, cost, and safety. I'm building RL environments where agents have to plan, reason about consequences, and handle failure modes — not just emit plausible-looking output.
